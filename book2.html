The Bower

80 pages | 5 1/2 x 8 1/2 | © 2019 Phoenix Poets
How can a person come to understand wars and hatreds well enough to explain them
truthfully to a child? The Bower engages this timeless and thorny question through
a recounting of the poet-speaker’s year in Belfast, Northern Ireland, with her young daughter.
The speaker immerses herself in the history of Irish politics—including the sectarian conflict
known as The Troubles—and gathers stories of a painful, divisive past from museum exhibits,
newspapers, neighbors, friends, local musicians, and cabbies. Quietly meditative, brooding,
and heart-wrenching, these poems place intimate moments between mother and daughter alongside
images of nationalistic violence and the angers that underlie our daily interactions.
A deep dive into sectarianism and forgiveness, this timely and nuanced book examines the many
ways we are all implicated in the impulse to “protect our own” and asks how we manage the histories that divide us.
